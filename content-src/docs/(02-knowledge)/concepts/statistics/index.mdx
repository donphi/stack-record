## What this map is

This map organises statistical concepts: inference, uncertainty, and models for data-generating processes. It is the place to land when a paper or pipeline mentions a test, a prior, or a likelihood and you want the local definition and context. The goal is practical orientation, not a textbook replacement.

## Why this matters

Experimental and observational work everywhere depends on honest uncertainty and comparable procedures. When statistics notes are scattered, people reinvent priors, p-values, and calibration language in incompatible ways. A single map keeps terminology aligned with how we actually evaluate systems and studies.

## Where this fits

Statistics bridges mathematics, machine learning, biomedical inference, and operations (experiments, decisions). Cross-links should favour short notes with examples tied to our stack and datasets.
