## What this map is

This map covers how raw inputs become vectors or sequences of vectors that downstream models consume: embeddings, encoders, and geometry of learned spaces. It is the conceptual home for “what is being represented” before you argue about architecture or training recipes. Use it when language, graphs, or multimodal stacks all claim to “embed” something.

## Why this matters

Representation choices determine what similarity means, what can be retrieved, and what failure modes look like in production. When representations are documented here, we can trace a bad ranking or drift symptom back to the intended geometry and invariants. That saves debugging time across retrieval, monitoring, and evaluation.

## Where this fits

Representation learning sits between core ML concepts and information-retrieval maps, and it touches systems notes on feature stores and embedding pipelines. Prefer links to small notes per representation family rather than model zoo pages.
