## What this map is

The domain map organizes every concept note in Stack Record by knowledge area. Each domain is a subfolder under `concepts/` containing atomic idea notes that define, explain, and connect the theoretical building blocks you reason with.

This is the "what you know" layer. Methods use these concepts. Systems implement them. Projects combine them. But the concepts themselves live here, grouped into six domains that reflect the intellectual territory of your work.

## Why this matters

Without domain grouping, concept notes pile up in a flat list. With 30% of your knowledge captured and twenty years ahead, flat breaks fast. The six domains below emerged from the topics you actually work on — they are not an abstract taxonomy imposed from outside.

Each domain answers a different kind of question:

- **Mathematics** answers: what are the formal objects and operations? Vectors, norms, inner products, distance metrics, dimensionality.
- **Statistics** answers: how do you reason about distributions and uncertainty? Jenks natural breaks, confusion matrices, F1 optimization, corpus statistics.
- **Information Retrieval** answers: how do you find, rank, and compare items? BM25, TF-IDF, cosine similarity, dot product, vector similarity, approximate nearest neighbors.
- **Representation Learning** answers: how do discrete things become continuous geometry? Embeddings, manifolds, Word2Vec, BERT, contextual representations, the corpus statistics gap.
- **Machine Learning** answers: how do models learn and generalize? Gradient descent, loss functions, batch tuning, model evaluation, the compression-expressiveness tradeoff.
- **Biomedical** answers: what domain-specific knowledge structures exist? UMLS concept identifiers, MONDO ontology, disease extraction, NER ensemble design.
