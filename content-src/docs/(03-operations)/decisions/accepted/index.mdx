## What this map is

Architecture Decision Records (ADRs) that were evaluated, approved, and implemented. These represent committed choices that shape the current system.

## Why this matters

Accepted decisions are the ground truth for "why is the system built this way?" Revisiting them prevents re-litigating settled questions and surfaces the constraints that led here.

## Where this fits

Accepted ADRs feed into [[systems]], [[methods]], and [[projects]] as the rationale behind their design. They are promoted from [[pending]] and contrast with [[rejected]] decisions.

## Structure

Each ADR follows a standard format: context, decision, consequences, and status.

## What this map helps answer

- Why was a particular technology or pattern chosen?
- What trade-offs were accepted and what was ruled out?
- When should an accepted decision be revisited?
