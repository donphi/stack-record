## What this map is

This map gathers biomedical concepts we rely on when normalising text, coding entities, or reasoning about phenotypes and diseases in pipelines. It is the conceptual layer above raw ontologies and below project-specific study notes. Use it when MeSH, disease labels, or clinical variables appear in design docs.

## Why this matters

Biomedical naming is noisy and politically loaded; small mismatches propagate into retrieval, cohort definitions, and compliance arguments. When we anchor terms here, we can say which ontology or coding system we mean and how it maps to features in code. That reduces silent relabelling across UKB, literature, and internal corpora.

## Where this fits

Biomedical concepts connect to information retrieval (entity linking), governance appendices, and operations projects that touch health data. Links should prefer stable identifiers and short explanations of how we use each resource in this organisation.

## Gaps and open loops

We still need explicit coverage of disease ontologies (e.g. hierarchy, equivalence, versioning) and of phenotype representation (EHR-derived versus survey-derived, normalisation, time). Genetic and variant-level concepts remain mostly out of scope until a project demands them.

## Review
