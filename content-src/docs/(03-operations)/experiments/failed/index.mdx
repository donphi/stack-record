## What this map is

Experiments that produced negative or null results and were not pursued further. These are documented failures, not missing data.

## Why this matters

Failed experiments are institutional memory. Recording what did not work — and why — saves future effort and often reveals constraints that successful experiments obscure.

## Where this fits

Failed experiments contrast with [[completed]] results and may resurface as [[pending]] decisions when conditions change. They draw on the same [[methods]] and [[evaluation]] frameworks.

## Structure

Each failed experiment records: hypothesis, method, what went wrong, and lessons learned.

## What this map helps answer

- What approaches have already been tried and abandoned?
- Why did a specific method fail in our context?
- Are there patterns in failure that suggest a deeper issue?

## Gaps and open loops

- Keep recording negative results even when they feel obvious
- Cross-reference failures with related successful experiments
- Revisit old failures when new tools or data become available
